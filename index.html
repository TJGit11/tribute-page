Frankie Knuckles

The Godfather of House Music

  • 1955 - Born Francis Warren Jr. in the Bronx borough of New York City
  • 1973 - Attends Fashion Institute of Technology to pursue textile design, while moonlighting as a DJ at disco clubs
  • 1970s - Towards the end of the '70s, Frankie Knuckles moves to Chicago
  • 1977 - Began performing regularly at The Warehouse in Chicago where he would develop his style of that would eventually become "house" music
  • 1982 - After years gaining popularity at The Warehouse, Frankie opens his own club in Chicago-- The Power Plant
  • 1983 - Frankie adds a drum machine to his toolkit and adds yet another layer to the iconic house music sound
  • 1991 - Releases debut studio album "Beyond the Mix"
  • 1995 - Releases second studio album "Welcome to the Real world"
  • 2014 - Frankie Knuckles dies of complications from type 2 diabetes

"The minute you think you're greater than the music, you're finished."

--Frankie Knuckles
